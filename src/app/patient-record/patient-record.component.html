Your health information is protected under the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). The hospital may use your health information for treatment, billing, and healthcare operations. Under HIPAA, you have the right to request restrictions on how your health information is used or disclosed, particularly for these purposes. While the hospital is not required to agree to such restrictions, it must comply if the request limits disclosures to a health plan for payment or healthcare operations and pertains to a service or item for which you or someone on your behalf has fully paid out-of-pocket. If the hospital agrees to your requested restrictions, it will adhere to the agreement except in certain situations, such as emergencies where your health information is necessary.
